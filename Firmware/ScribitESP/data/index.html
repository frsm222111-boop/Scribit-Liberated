Our Story

        
The company quit.
Your Scribit didn't.

        
Scribit was sold as a connected wall-drawing robot — and it only ever worked through the maker's cloud. Then they moved on. The app and servers went dark, the website lapsed, and thousands of robots people paid hundreds of dollars for turned into bricks overnight. No support. No refunds. No way to use the thing you already owned.

      


      

        

          01
          
What we did

          
We cut the cord. Scribit Liberated reflashes your robot to run 100% locally — no cloud, no account, no permission. We fixed the drawing so lines come out straight, rebuilt the entire control panel in your browser, and added tools the original never had: generative art, photo-to-line-art, text plotting, and a guided calibration wizard.

        

        

          02
          
No more limits

          
The factory nail-template is gone. Mount the anchors at any distance that fits your wall and just measure it once. Your robot, your space, your rules — the way it should have been from day one.

        

        

          03
          
Where we stand

          
Open source. Pay what you want. No lock-in, no telemetry, nothing phoning home. If a company can brick something you bought, you never really owned it — so we gave it back. They bet you'd give up. You didn't.

        

      


      
Built on the open-source UnBrickIt project — and on every owner who refused to throw a working robot in a drawer. Every Scribit deserves to keep drawing.
